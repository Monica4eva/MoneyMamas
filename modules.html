Module 1: Money Mindset & Goal-Setting

“If you don’t question your money story, you’ll repeat it forever.”

Unpacking Limiting Beliefs

  • Scarcity vs. Abundance: Ask yourself: “Do I really believe there’s never enough?”
    Exercise: List three times you told yourself “I can’t afford it.” Then reframe each: “I choose to…”
  • Family Money Narratives: Map out your earliest money memories. Who said “money doesn’t grow on trees”?
    Poetic prompt: Write a one-sentence “family money mantra” you’re ready to retire.

Crafting Family-Focused Visions

  • Vision Boarding: Old-school magazine cutouts vs. digital Pinterest boards—pick your style.
  • SMART Goals, Mama Style: “Save $300 by end of month” beats vague “I want more savings.”
  • Mini-challenge: Draft a 6-word mission statement for your household’s money vibe.

Module 2: Budgeting Basics (Then & Now)

“Budgets aren’t prison sentences—think of them as love letters to your future self.”

Envelope System vs. Modern Apps

  • Envelope: tactile, visceral—feel that paper cash.
  • Apps (Tala, Moya Wallet): auto-sync, notifications, cute charts.
  • Debate: Which feels more empowering for you?

Zero-Based Budget Walkthrough

  • Every pula has a job: groceries, school fees, “fun fund.”
  • Live demo: Build a zero-based budget in a spreadsheet (or app) with sample numbers.

Hands-On Exercise

  • Allocate last month’s income line-by-line. Where did you overspend? What surprised you?

Module 3: Expense Tracking & Analysis

“You can’t fix a leak if you don’t know where the drip is.”

Manual Ledgers vs. Auto-Synced Feeds

  • Pen-and-paper: heightened awareness, no tech headaches.
  • Feeds (MTN Excelerator, SCB app): real-time balance, trend graphs.

Identifying “Leak Points”

  • Drill-down: Netflix subs you forgot, random airtime top-ups, “just one more” deliveries.
  • Group brainstorm: Share your sneakiest leak and how you’d seal it.

Data Deep-Dive

  • Quick pivot-table demo: Categorize last 10 transactions; spot the biggest category.

Module 4: Building an Emergency Fund

“Future-proofing isn’t optional—it’s survival.”

Three-to-Six Months Rule

  • Traditional logic: living expenses × 3–6.
  • Skeptic’s corner: Is that realistic on a hectic single-mom income?

Micro-Savings Hacks

  • Round-up apps: spare change on every swipe.
  • “No-coffee” challenge: skip one fancy latté per week; stash $15.

Action Plan

  • Set a small first milestone (e.g., $500); celebrate on Slack/WhatsApp group.

Module 5: Debt Management & Repair

“Debt is a tool—wield it, don’t be wielded by it.”

Snowball vs. Avalanche

  • Snowball: small debts first = instant wins.
  • Avalanche: highest-rate first = math-optimal.
  • Exercise: Calculate which saves you the most in interest.

Negotiating & Consolidations

  • Script: “Can you lower my rate by X%? I’m a loyal customer.”
  • Pros/cons of consolidation loans in your particular country: watch the fine print.

Credit Repair Tips

  • Dispute one small error on your report each month—to keep momentum.

Module 6: Credit Score Mastery

“Your score is a mirror—time to polish.”

Reading Your Report

  • Inquiries, payment history, public records.
  • Warning flags: unknown inquiries, late payments older than 2 years.

Time-Tested Habits

  • Pay on or before due date—set calendar alerts.
  • Keep credit-utilization under 30%.

Myth-Busting

  • Closing an old card can hurt more than help.
  • Mini-quiz: True or false?

Module 7: Savings & Investing Fundamentals

“Let your money gossip: ‘Guess what I did last night?’”

Savings vs. Bonds vs. Stocks

  • Savings account: ultra-safe, low yield.
  • Government bonds: moderate yield, moderate lock-in.
  • Stocks (local brokers/apps): high-risk/high-reward.

Compound Interest

  • “Magic multiplier” demo: P250 → growth over 10 years at 5% annually.

Portfolio Primer

  • Simple 60/40 split: 60% low-risk, 40% growth.
  • Micro-investing platforms: start with P100.

Module 8: Retirement & Future-Proofing

“Retirement isn’t a someday dream—it’s a daily drip.”

Pension Basics

  • BPOPF vs. private schemes: vesting periods, employer match, portability.

Starting Small

  • $500–$1000 monthly via unit trusts or annuities.
  • “Set-and-forget” standing orders.

Visualization Exercise

  • Imagine yourself at 65—what do you want your nest egg to cover? Write it down.

Module 9: Side Hustles for Moms

“Hustle smart, not hustle hard.”

Idea Generation

  • Craft sales, digital freelancing, tutoring.
  • Market test: pre-sell one prototype/service to a friend.

Time/Energy/Profit Matrix

  • Chart potential hustles by setup time vs. ongoing energy.

Mini-Plan

  • Draft a one-week action plan: Day 1—outline service; Day 2—price it.

Module 10: Insurance & Protection

“Peace of mind is the ultimate luxury.”

Life & Health Insurance 101

  • Term life vs. endowment. Hospital plans: network vs. out-of-pocket.

Micro-Insurance

  • Airtime-based premiums for low-income households: pros/cons.

Family Safeguards

  • Check your nominee updates—when was yours last reviewed?

Module 11: Digital Tools & Security

“Hackers never nap; neither should your defenses.”

Password Hygiene

  • Passphrases: four random words > “P@ssw0rd1!” Use a manager (Bitwarden, KeePass).

Two-Factor Auth (2FA)

  • Authenticator apps vs. SMS. Set up on banking & email today.

Top-5 Money Apps

  • Budgeting: YNAB vs. Goodbudget. Investing: EasyEquities, GC Funds.

Module 12: Teaching Money to Kids

“Plant a seed of financial wisdom; watch them bloom.”

Storytelling & Allowances

  • “Spend, save, share” jars. Picture-book narratives about choices.

Chore-Based Rewards

  • Assign value to chores; track on a chore chart.

Age-Appropriate Lessons

  • 4–6 yrs: coin-sorting games. 7–10 yrs: basic ledgers. 11+ yrs: budget their “fun fund.”

Module 13: Negotiation & Earning Power

“If you don’t ask, the answer’s always no.”

Raise-Scripts that Work

  • “I’ve driven X outcomes; based on market data, a Y adjustment feels fair.”

Reframing Money Talks

  • Role-play partner conversations: “Let’s align on our shared goals.”

Confidence-Boosters

  • Power-pose before the ask; journal three wins.

Module 14: Legacy & Estate Basics

“Your story deserves a proper closing chapter.”

Wills & Beneficiaries

  • DIY will kits vs. legal counsel. Update nominees on policies/accounts.

Passing on Wealth

  • Customs (lobola, trusts) vs. statutory frameworks. Plan “estate talk” this month.
